Programs

FORMAL STUDENT TRAINING

3 selections:
  1. $700​ for 4 weeks: ​8 hours a week. Includes 2 hours of private lesson (1 hour with student’s main coach and 1 hour with another coach at club’s discretion), 2 hours of serve practice and physical training, and 4 hours of group lesson.
  2. $900 for 4 weeks: ​10 hours a week. Includes 4 hours of private lesson (2 hours with student’s main coach and 2 hours with another coach at club’s discretion), 2 hours of serve practice and physical training, and 4 hours of group lesson.
  3. $1100 for 4 weeks: ​13 hours a week. Includes 6 hours of private lesson (3 hours with student’s main coach and 3 hours with another coach at club’s discretion), 3 hours of serve practice and physical training, and 4 hours of group lesson.

*Physical training and serve practice will have a coach watching at all times. Physical training and serve practice times will be determined by the club. Exact times to be decided. If student does not want to participate in serve and physical training, the price will be $100 less than the price above.

FORMAL STUDENT BENEFITS

  1. Students who qualify for the National Team will have additional lessons added for free and prize money (refreshes every year):
    • Qualifying for the Hopes Team, Mini Cadet National Team, and Cadet National Team - 1 additional private lesson every week (assigned coach will be determined by club)
    • Qualifying for Junior National Team - 2 additional private lessons every week (assigned coach will be determined by club)
    • Qualifying for World Junior Championship - 2 additional private lessons every week (assigned coach will be determined by club) and each student will receive $500 prize money (50% will go to the student and 50% to the student’s main coach)
    • Qualifying for the World Championship or World Cup - 2 additional private lessons every week (assigned coach will be determined by club) and each student will receive $1000 prize money (50% will go to the student and 50% to the student’s main coach)
    • Qualifying for Youth Olympics and Olympics - 2 additional private lessons every week (assigned coach will be determined by club) and each student will receive $3000 prize money (50% will go to the student and 50% to the student’s main coach)
    • Reaching top 4 in US National or US Open Men’s or Women’s Singles - 2 additional private lessons every week (assigned coach will be determined by club) and each student will receive $1000 prize money (50% will go to the student and 50% to the student’s main coach)
    • * Note: There is a maximum of 2 additional private lessons every week for a student regardless of however many accomplishments above are achieved. No additional fee needs to be paid for the additional private lessons.
  2. Students will receive a replacement DHS blade every 1-2 years, 1-4 sheets of Hurricane rubber every year (only for formal students who have trained here for at least 3 months).
  3. Free club clothing (only for formal students who have trained here for at least 3 months).
  4. All students will have a main coach assigned to them.

FORMAL STUDENT RULES

  1. Once you become a formal student, you must pay for a $500 formal student fee (only need to be paid once).
  2. Each student must pay a $300 fee for each US Nationals and a $300 fee for each US Open. This money will be used to pay for the coaches to coach the students during the tournament. The coaches will be there to coach the whole tournament (however there is no guarantee that every match will have a coach there to coach the student).
  3. For smaller tournaments, if student would like coaches to coach them during the tournament, each student can pay a $100 fee for each day.
  4. Student must wear club attire when playing any tournament. If a student is caught violating this, club will determine penalty.
  5. During training, parents can not enter the training area to pick up balls or interfere with the training. Parents can talk to the coach before and after the training session.
  6. Students should show up on time. If they are late, no extra time will be given to them for that training session.
  7. There is a maximum number of formal students that the club can accept. The club will determine the specific number.
  8. If formal student is seen giving up during training or a match, the club has the power to remove them from being a formal student effective immediately
* Parents can remove their child from being a formal student at any time effective immediately.

NON-FORMAL STUDENT TRAINING

2 selections:
  1. $800​ for 4 weeks: ​7 hours a week. Includes 3 hours of private lesson (1 hour with student’s coach of choice and 2 with another coach at club’s discretion) and 4 hours of group lesson.
  2. $1000​ for 4 weeks: ​9 hours a week. Includes 5 hours of private lesson (2 hour with student’s coach of choice and 3 with another coach at club’s discretion) and 4 hours of group lesson.

NON-FORMAL STUDENT RULES

  1. Students should show up on time. If they are late, no extra time will be given to them for that training session.
  2. Students do not need to pay for membership fee. Club will only provide training to the student.
  3. Student will not receive clothing, paddles, rubber, prize money, and coaching at tournaments from the coaches. They are allowed to represent any club they want at tournaments.
  4. If all coaches are completely booked and formal students need to add lessons, non-formal students must give up their training slot to formal students (at the club’s discretion).

Other Notes

  • Outside of formal and non-formal students, if other people would like to attend the group training on Saturday and/or Sunday, each training session is 2 hours and is $40 for one day, $75 for 2 days, and $260 for 4 weeks of group training. Group training time will be determined.
  • Club will go through trial period starting June 8, 2017
  • After the trial period ends, there will be a big tournament every 3 months with $5000 prize pool. There will also be a small tournament every 2 weeks with prize pool between $300-800 (to be decided). These tournaments will not count for USATT rating. Specific dates/times to be decided.
  • If any problems occur related to what is stated above or other problems come up, the club will have final say.
